CASUALITY

A casualty in a hospital refers to a patient who has suffered an injury, illness, or trauma that requires immediate medical attention.

This term is often used in emergency departments to categorize patients who need urgent care, including accidents, heart attacks, severe infections, or other critical conditions.

GENERAL MEDICINE

General medicine in a hospital focuses on diagnosing, treating, and preventing a wide range of adult diseases and medical conditions.

It encompasses various specialties, including cardiology, endocrinology, and respiratory medicine. General physicians provide comprehensive care, coordinate treatments, and manage long-term health conditions, ensuring holistic patient well-being.

GENERAL SURGERY

General surgery in a hospital involves a wide range of procedures to treat conditions affecting the abdomen, digestive tract, skin, and soft tissues.

Surgeons perform operations for injuries, diseases, and infections, focusing on both emergency and elective surgeries to improve patients' health and quality of life.

ORTHOPAEDIC

Orthopaedic care in hospitals focuses on diagnosing, treating, and preventing conditions related to the bones, joints, ligaments, tendons, and muscles.

This includes injuries, degenerative diseases, deformities, and fractures. Treatment may involve surgery, physical therapy, or other interventions aimed at improving mobility, pain relief, and overall musculoskeletal health.

GYNECOLOGY

Gynecology is a medical specialty focusing on the female reproductive system, including the uterus, ovaries, and vagina.

In hospitals, gynecologists diagnose and treat conditions such as menstrual disorders, pregnancy-related issues, infertility, and cancers. They also provide preventive care, screenings, and perform surgeries to ensure women's reproductive health.

E.N.T

The E.N.T (Ear, Nose, and Throat) department in a hospital provides specialized care for disorders related to the head and neck, including hearing loss, sinus problems, and throat conditions.

It offers diagnostic, medical, and surgical treatments to manage conditions such as infections, allergies, and cancers affecting these areas.



DERMATOLOGY

Dermatology in a hospital focuses on diagnosing and treating skin disorders, including conditions like acne, eczema, and skin cancer.

With specialized medical and surgical interventions, dermatologists provide care for both cosmetic and health-related skin issues, ensuring patient well-being and improving quality of life.

PEDIATRICIAN

A pediatrician in a hospital specializes in diagnosing and treating health conditions in children, from infancy through adolescence.

They provide preventive care, manage chronic conditions, and offer guidance to parents on child development, nutrition, and vaccinations, ensuring the overall well-being of young patients.

MODERN LAB

The Modern Lab in a hospital is equipped with advanced technology to provide accurate and rapid diagnostic services.

It ensures efficient patient care by offering a wide range of tests, from routine blood work to specialized analyses, supporting doctors in making informed treatment decisions.

RADIOLOGY

Radiology in a hospital involves using imaging technologies such as X-rays, CT scans, MRIs, and ultrasounds to diagnose and monitor various medical conditions.

It plays a crucial role in detecting injuries, diseases, and abnormalities, aiding in accurate diagnosis and treatment planning for patients.

ANESTHESIA

Anesthesia is a medical treatment that prevents patients from feeling pain during surgery or other medical procedures by inducing a controlled state of unconsciousness or numbness.

It is administered by anesthesiologists who carefully monitor the patient's vital signs to ensure safety and comfort throughout the procedure.

CARDIOLOGY

Cardiology in a hospital focuses on diagnosing, treating, and preventing heart-related conditions, including diseases of the heart, blood vessels, and circulatory system.

It involves a range of procedures, from non-invasive tests to complex surgeries, aimed at improving cardiovascular health and managing conditions like heart attacks, arrhythmias, and hypertension.

NEURO MEDICINE

Neuro medicine in hospitals focuses on diagnosing and treating neurological disorders such as strokes, epilepsy, and neurodegenerative diseases.

Specialized treatments and therapies, including advanced imaging and surgical interventions, are provided to manage conditions affecting the brain, spine, and nervous system to improve patient outcomes and quality of life.

NEURO SURGERY

Neurosurgery is a specialized field of medicine focused on the surgical treatment of disorders affecting the brain, spine, and nervous system.

It involves procedures to address conditions such as tumors, trauma, vascular malformations, and degenerative diseases. Neurosurgeons use advanced techniques to improve patient outcomes and quality of life.

O.M.F

OMF (Open Manufacturing Format) is an open-source, flexible data format designed to support communication between different manufacturing systems.

It facilitates seamless data exchange, enabling manufacturers to improve automation, optimize workflows, and enhance interoperability between software tools in the production process. OMF aims to streamline manufacturing operations and boost efficiency.

NEONATOLOGY

Neonatology is a specialized branch of pediatrics that focuses on the care of newborn infants, particularly those who are premature, ill, or require intensive medical attention.

Neonatologists provide comprehensive care to ensure the survival and health of newborns, managing conditions such as respiratory issues, infections, and congenital disorders.

DIETICIAN

A dietitian is a healthcare professional who specializes in nutrition and dietary planning.

They assess individual nutritional needs, provide personalized advice, and develop meal plans to promote health, manage chronic conditions, or aid in recovery. Dietitians work with clients to improve overall well-being through proper nutrition and lifestyle changes.

PALLIATIVE CARE

Palliative care is specialized medical care focused on providing relief from the symptoms, pain, and stress of serious illness.

It aims to improve quality of life for patients and their families, regardless of the patient's age or stage of the illness, by addressing physical, emotional, and spiritual needs.




3 LAMINAR OPERATION THEATRES

The 3 Laminar Operation Theatres are advanced surgical environments designed to provide a sterile and controlled atmosphere for complex medical procedures.

Equipped with high-efficiency particulate air (HEPA) filters, these theatres maintain clean air circulation, reducing the risk of infection and ensuring patient safety during surgeries.

I.C.U

I.C.U (Intensive Care Unit) is a specialized hospital department designed to provide intensive treatment and monitoring for patients with severe or life-threatening conditions.

It is equipped with advanced medical technology and staffed by healthcare professionals, ensuring round-the-clock care for critical patients requiring constant observation and support.

AMBULANCE

An ambulance is a specially equipped vehicle designed to transport injured or sick individuals to medical facilities.

It is staffed by trained emergency medical personnel who provide critical care during transit. Ambulances are equipped with life-saving equipment like defibrillators, oxygen tanks, and stretchers to stabilize patients.

ICU AMBULANCE

ICU Ambulance provides critical emergency medical transportation with state-of-the-art equipment and highly trained medical staff.

Equipped with advanced life support systems, it ensures the highest level of care for patients during transport. The service is available 24/7, offering rapid response and professional medical assistance in emergencies.

MORTUARY

The mortuary in a hospital is a designated facility for the storage and care of deceased individuals before burial or cremation.

It ensures respectful handling of the body, maintaining hygiene and dignity, while also providing a space for identification, autopsy, and legal procedures if necessary.

MOBILE MORTUARY

A mobile mortuary in a hospital is a specialized, portable facility designed to store and manage deceased bodies in emergency or remote situations.

It ensures respectful handling and proper preservation of the deceased while supporting hospitals with limited space or during high-demand times.

FITNESS CLUB(GYM)

Emergency medicine specializes The fitness club within the hospital offers a state-of-the-art gym facility designed to promote physical well-being and recovery.

Equipped with modern equipment and expert trainers, it provides personalized fitness programs to enhance patients' rehabilitation and overall health in a supportive, healing environment.

HOSTEL FACILITIES

The hospital offers comfortable and well-equipped hostel facilities for patients' attendants, ensuring a convenient and supportive environment during their stay.

These facilities include clean rooms, 24/7 security, and access to essential amenities, providing a safe and comfortable space for family members while their loved ones receive care.
